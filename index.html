About Our MUN

What is Model United Nations?

Model United Nations (MUN) is a transformative academic simulation where students step into roles of world leaders, diplomats, and international policymakers. It is a high-stakes arena of debate and negotiation, tasking participants with resolving the most pressing crises facing our global community.

Far more than a mere exercise in public speaking, MUN challenges students to navigate the complexities of international relations, master the art of diplomacy, and forge consensus amidst conflicting national interests. It is where the classroom meets the world stage, turning students into active architects of the future.

Our Goals at KKIS MUN

At KKIS MUN, we do not just host a conference; we forge the next generation of global leaders. Our mission is fueled by a profound pride in our students' potential to shape history. We are dedicated to developing elite young diplomats who possess the tactical brilliance to negotiate and the moral courage to lead.

We aim to cultivate commanding public speakers whose voices carry the weight of conviction and clarity of reason. At KKIS, we empower our delegates to master critical thinking and strategic collaboration, ensuring they leave our halls not just as participants, but as visionary thinkers ready to command the international stage with excellence and integrity.

The Diplomatic Toolkit

🎤

Mastery of Public Speaking

Delegates learn to command the room, moving beyond mere talking to deliver persuasive, structured, and authoritative speeches that capture the attention of the entire General Assembly.

🤝

Strategic Negotiation

The art of "win-win" is central to diplomacy. Participants develop the ability to navigate conflicting interests, build powerful coalitions, and bridge the gap between opposing national agendas.

📝

Analytical Research & Policy Writing

Success in committee starts with deep preparation. Delegates master the skill of dissecting complex global issues and translating their findings into formal, professional policy resolutions.

🧠

Critical Thinking & Problem Solving

When a crisis hits the floor, there is no script. Our delegates learn to think on their feet, analyzing real-time data to create innovative solutions to the world's most volatile challenges.

🌐

Global Cultural Intelligence

Representing a nation requires stepping outside of one's own perspective. Participants gain a sophisticated understanding of international relations, diverse cultures, and various geopolitical histories.

⚖️

Formal Leadership & Ethics

Governed by Rules of Procedure, delegates practice high-level professional decorum. They learn to lead with integrity, managing teams and committees with a sense of "wise and considerate" authority.

Conference Schedule

Day One: Opening & Deliberation

Wednesday, April 22nd, 2026 | 6:00 PM – 11:00 PM

🎭

Opening Ceremony

The formal introduction to the conference, featuring keynote addresses to inspire the weekend's debate.

📋

Committee Session I: Speaker's List

The floor opens for Roll Call and Opening Speeches, where delegates first present their country's official positions.

💬

Committee Session II: Moderated Caucus

A structured debate where the committee discusses specific sub-topics and begins exploring potential solutions.

☕

Diplomatic Break

A scheduled intermission for refreshments and informal networking between delegates.

✍️

Drafting Working Papers

The first stage of the resolution process, where delegates begin putting their ideas into official written format.

Day Two: Resolution & Closing

Thursday, April 23rd, 2026 | 6:00 PM – 11:00 PM

🤝

Committee Session III: Unmoderated Caucus

The final lobbying period. Delegates work freely in groups to merge papers and finalize their proposed resolutions.

🗳️

Voting Procedures & Adoption

The definitive phase of the conference where proposed resolutions are formally voted upon by the committee.

❓

Executive Q&A & Guest Reception

An interactive session with the Executive Board and invited guests to discuss the outcomes of the debate.

🏆

Closing Ceremony & Awards

The final assembly to recognize outstanding diplomatic performance and officially conclude the conference.
